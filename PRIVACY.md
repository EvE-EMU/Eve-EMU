# Privacy policy

**Last updated:** May 12, 2026

This policy describes how the **EVE Emu** project ([EvE-EMU/Eve-EMU](https://github.com/EvE-EMU/Eve-EMU)) handles information when you use our software, services we operate, or this repository.

For rules of use, acceptable conduct, and CCP / licensing notices, see **[TERMS.md](TERMS.md)**.

---

## Summary

We build and maintain **localized datasets** derived from **EVE Online**—including, for example, **character names**, **Static Data Export (SDE)** item definitions, and **market data**—to power emulation and related tools. We do **not** sell personal data, use it for cross-site advertising, or build unrelated marketing profiles.

---

## What we process and why

### Game and emulation datasets

The project maintains **local or project-controlled databases** of EVE-related data needed for core functionality, including but not limited to:

- **Character-related data** (for example names and identifiers surfaced through official APIs such as ESI, where used).
- **SDE-derived data** (items, types, and related static game definitions).
- **Market data** (pricing and related market feeds where integrated).

This processing exists to **provide, operate, and improve** the EVE Emu experience (for example emulation accuracy, tooling, and diagnostics), not for unrelated commercial profiling.

### Authentication and sessions

Where the software uses **EVE SSO / ESI** or similar mechanisms, we process **tokens and identifiers** only as needed for authentication and authorized API access. Treat API keys and tokens as **secrets**; do not commit them to public repositories.

### Cookies and analytics (web deployments)

If you use a **web-based** deployment of this project (for example a site or dashboard maintained by the project), that deployment **may** use cookies or similar technologies for **session management** and **basic operational analytics**. You can often control cookies through your browser; disabling them may limit sign-in or certain features.

**Repository-only use:** If you only browse or clone this GitHub repository, ordinary processing is governed by **GitHub’s** policies, not ours. See the [GitHub Privacy Statement](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-privacy-statement).

---

## What we do not do

- We do **not** sell your personal data.
- We do **not** use your data for targeted advertising across unrelated sites.
- We do **not** claim ownership of **CCP’s** game data; see **Data ownership** below.

---

## Data ownership (CCP vs. project)

**EVE Online** and related names, logos, ships, lore, and other game assets are the intellectual property of **CCP hf.** Game-derived data (characters, corporations, alliances, market listings as presented in-game, etc.) remains subject to **CCP’s** rights and terms.

This project is a **third-party emulation effort** and is **not** affiliated with, endorsed, or sponsored by CCP hf. For privacy or legal questions about **CCP-held** data, contact CCP through their official channels (for example [legal@ccpgames.com](mailto:legal@ccpgames.com) where appropriate for GDPR-related inquiries to the data controller).

**Project-held** preferences, account settings, or deployment-specific records (where applicable) are managed by whoever operates that deployment; see **Your rights** below.

---

## Your rights and choices

Depending on your jurisdiction (including the **GDPR** where it applies), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain processing, or to object to certain uses.

- For data held by **CCP** about your EVE Online account, contact **CCP**.
- For data held by a **specific EVE Emu deployment** (self-hosted or community-operated), contact the **operators** of that deployment or the maintainers via the channels published in this repository (for example GitHub issues).

---

## Security

We aim to handle credentials and API access responsibly. **Report security vulnerabilities** through coordinated disclosure (for example a private security advisory or maintainer contact), not via public exploit posts.

---

## Self-hosted and third-party operators

Anyone may run modified or self-hosted copies of this software. **Those operators** (not necessarily the upstream maintainers) are responsible for their own logs, databases, backups, and compliance. This policy describes the **project’s** intent and typical practices; a particular instance may publish its own notice.

If project-operated services are hosted in multiple regions, data **may** be processed in countries other than your own. We rely on appropriate safeguards where required by law.
